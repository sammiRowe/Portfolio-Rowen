How might we make tired and overworked parents feel confident their children will lead healthy lifestyles?

Especially if the parents weren’t even leading these themselves? When the Covid-19 pandemic shut the doors on society as we knew it, parents took on new roles as a teacher, a friend, an entertainer, all while continuing on with their career and, not to mention, their continuous role as role-model and parent. Children were looking even closer at their parents’ daily habits, and without the stimulation of school and social activities, many parents worried the habits their children formed during this unconventional time would alter their lives forever.

The Team

We were tasked with creating a convenience for families during quarantine—whether that took shape digitally or physically.

The Interviews.

We interviewed 5 parents, over Zoom, about their experience during quarantine. These interviews took place in Spring of 2021, when some of the severity of the shelter-in-place orders were being lifted, but when many still felt uncomfortable completely assimilating back into society.

And the interviews were telling. The parents shared their struggles through body language and deep sighs, through the retelling of stories, and even through where they chose to interview, with one mother interviewing in her car while on a lunch break. Two of our parents, a single mother and a single father, worked in healthcare throughout the pandemic, adding an even deeper layer in which to discover pain points and possibility.

Interesting Finds & Notable Trends from the Interviewees:

  • Emphases on this being a special time in their children’s lives, despite the children’s age ranging from 6 months to teenagers.
  • Major focus on building and maintaining stronger relationships and participating in quality time together.
  • They enjoyed the ‘slowness’ of quarantine—felt in control of their lives without the pressure of peers (both for the parents and the children).
  • A shock—most parents saw the shelter in place as an overall positive experience.

Direct Quotes from our Interviewees

“We had a rule — you couldn’t be in your room for longer than 4 hours during quarantine.”

“Fresh air does a lot for mental health.”

“It was a free for all for me, we can go skiing and [her son] can just log in on the parking lot or on the ski lift — we can do more things we enjoy rather than school.”

“I ordered groceries to be delivered home, that was a first.”

“We were able to teach [our daughter] the way we wanted to.”

“I bought a spin bike, which was something I used to do regularly. I bought it because I had to create my own joy.”
